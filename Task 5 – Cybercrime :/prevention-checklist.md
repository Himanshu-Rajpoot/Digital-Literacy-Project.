## Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist (For College Students)

### General Online Safety
- Think before posting anything online; once shared, it can be permanent
- Avoid sharing personal details like phone number, address, college ID, or location publicly
- Keep your social media accounts private and review privacy settings regularly
- Use strong, unique passwords for each account (avoid using the same password everywhere)
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for important accounts like email, banking, and social media
- Log out from shared or public computers after use
- Do not click on suspicious links sent via email, SMS, or social media
- Install apps only from trusted sources like Google Play Store or App Store
- Keep your device software and apps updated for security patches

### Cyberbullying Safety
- Do not reply to abusive or threatening messages; it may encourage the bully
- Block and report users who harass or bully you online
- Take screenshots and save evidence of cyberbullying (messages, posts, profiles)
- Talk to a trusted person (friend, parent, teacher, or counselor)
- Do not engage in spreading rumors or forwarding harmful content about others

### UPI & Financial Safety
- Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money; PIN is only required to send money
- Do not scan unknown QR codes or click on suspicious payment links
- Always verify payment requests before approving them in apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm
- Do not share OTP, UPI PIN, or bank details with anyone, even if they claim to be from the bank
- Check transaction details carefully before confirming any payment
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for financial transactions

### Device & Account Security
- Use screen lock (PIN, password, or fingerprint) on your phone and laptop
- Install reliable antivirus or security apps
- Regularly review account activity and login history
- Remove access to unused apps or third-party services

### Reporting Cybercrime (India)
- Report cybercrime at the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
- Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (especially for financial fraud)
- Inform your bank immediately in case of unauthorized transactions
- Report cyberbullying on the platform (Instagram, WhatsApp, etc.) using in-app reporting tools
